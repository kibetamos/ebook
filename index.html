About The Book

The Midnight Library by Matt Haig is a dazzling thought-provoking novel that explores the infinite possibilities of a single life. Between life and death lies a library filled with endless books—each one offering a chance to try out a different version of your existence.

In this genre-defying masterpiece, readers follow Nora Seed as she navigates regrets, alternate realities, and the haunting question: What if? Perfect for fans of Emily St. John Mandel and Fredrik Backman, this #1 New York Times bestseller has been translated into over 40 languages and is being adapted into a major film.

Introducing The Midnight Library

The Midnight Library by Matt Haig begins with Nora Seed, a woman who feels she has failed at everything. After a series of devastating losses, she decides she no longer wants to live.

What is The Midnight Library? Between life and death lies a magical library. Each book on the shelves contains a different life Nora could have lived—if she had made one different choice along the way.

"Between life and death there is a library, and within that library, the shelves go on forever." — Matt Haig

The novel explores regrets, mental health, and the profound realization that no life is perfect. But every life holds moments worth living for.

The Book of Regrets

When Nora first enters the Midnight Library, she meets Mrs. Elm, the librarian from her childhood. Mrs. Elm shows her the Book of Regrets—an endless volume listing every choice Nora wishes she had made differently.

From not marrying her ex-fiancé to abandoning her music career, Nora's regrets seem endless. But Mrs. Elm offers her a chance: to undo any of them.

The library holds books for lives where Nora became a glaciologist, a rock star, a mother, an Olympic swimmer, and dozens more. Each offers a glimpse of what could have been.

As Nora steps into alternate realities, she learns that every life carries its own burdens. No alternate version is without pain—or without beauty.

The Midnight Library

The library itself is a character in the story—a liminal space between sleeping and waking, living and dying. It contains not only books of lives lived but also books of lives that never were.

Matt Haig draws on his own struggles with depression and anxiety to craft a story that feels deeply personal yet universally resonant. The library offers Nora infinite beginnings.

Each new life she tries teaches her something: success without connection feels empty, love without understanding fades, and even her "worst" life held moments of joy she had forgotten.

A #1 New York Times Bestseller

Translated into over 40 languages and soon to be a major motion picture, The Midnight Library has touched millions of readers worldwide.

Alternate Lives

From becoming a philosophy professor to a pub owner, from a famous rock musician to a rescue swimmer—Nora tries on identities like costumes. Some feel right for a moment. Others feel impossible.

But each life teaches her something crucial about the life she actually lived. She learns that her brother loved her more than she knew. That her cat mattered. That one kind conversation can change everything.

As she experiences these parallels, Nora begins to understand that the root of her suffering wasn't failure—it was the belief that she had failed. The library lets her see her own life as others saw it.

Matt Haig reminds us that we are not defined by our worst moments—or our best ones. We are defined by what we do next.

The Only Way to Learn

By the end of her journey, Nora discovers the most important truth of all: there is no single perfect life. Every existence contains both tragedy and wonder. The only real regret is never realizing that.

The final chapters deliver a breathtaking emotional payoff. Nora must decide whether to return to her original life—the one she tried to end—or disappear into the vastness of the library forever.

Why this book matters: The Midnight Library is more than a novel. It's a meditation on depression, hope, and the quiet miracle of ordinary days. It has been recommended by therapists and beloved by book clubs around the world.

Meet The Author

Matt Haig

Matt Haig is the #1 New York Times bestselling author of The Midnight Library, The Humans, How to Stop Time, and the memoir Reasons to Stay Alive. His books have sold more than 3 million copies worldwide and have been translated into over 40 languages.

Haig draws deeply from his own experiences with depression and anxiety, bringing authentic emotional depth to his fiction. The Midnight Library was a Goodreads Choice Award winner for Fiction in 2020 and spent over 60 weeks on the New York Times bestseller list. He lives in Brighton, England, with his family.
